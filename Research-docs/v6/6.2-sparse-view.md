# Section 2: Sparse-View Generation Strategies (1-3 Cameras)

## Executive Summary

The v5 plan contains **two critical overstatements**: (1) that inference-time diffusion is an absolute technological dead-end, and (2) that LRM feed-forward transformers can flawlessly hallucinate occluded regions. Reality in 2025-2026: single-step and few-step distilled diffusion models (DiffusionGS, Difix3D+) achieve 5-10x speedups, making lightweight diffusion refinement feasible at near-real-time latency. Simultaneously, pure feed-forward LRMs produce blurry, averaged textures in occluded zones and suffer from near-field overfitting. The **recommended architecture** is a hybrid: feed-forward LRM base for speed + optional 1-step diffusion refinement for quality, following the GIFSplat/iLRM paradigm.

## SOTA Analysis 2025-2026

### 2.1 The New Landscape: Beyond Binary "Diffusion vs Feed-Forward"

The 2025-2026 research has dissolved the hard boundary between diffusion-based and feed-forward approaches. Three paradigms now coexist:

**Paradigm A: Pure Feed-Forward (LRM)**
- HumanSplat, Long-LRM, SHARP
- Latency: 0.3-1.0s
- Quality: Good for visible regions, degraded for occluded areas
- Best for: Real-time rendering pipeline initialization

**Paradigm B: Single-Stage Diffusion**
- DiffusionGS (ICCV 2025): Directly outputs 3D Gaussian point clouds at each timestep
- Latency: ~6s on A100 (5x faster than two-stage)
- Quality: +2.20 dB PSNR improvement over prior two-stage methods
- Best for: Near-real-time quality generation

**Paradigm C: Hybrid Feed-Forward + Refinement**
- GIFSplat (2026): Feed-forward base + iterative residual updates with distilled diffusion priors
- iLRM (2025): Two-stage attention with iterative refinement
- Difix3D+ (2025): Single-step diffusion for artifact cleanup on 3DGS renders
- Latency: 2-10s depending on refinement iterations
- Quality: Approaches optimization-based methods
- Best for: Our MVP — balances speed and quality

### 2.2 DiffusionGS — Single-Stage 3D Diffusion (ICCV 2025)

Key innovation: instead of generating 2D images and lifting to 3D, DiffusionGS directly outputs 3D Gaussian point clouds at each denoising timestep. This enforces view consistency structurally rather than through expensive cross-view attention.

- Input: Single image or text prompt
- Output: Complete 3D Gaussian scene
- Speed: ~6 seconds on NVIDIA A100
- Quality: PSNR +2.20 dB over prior art for objects
- Limitation: Still requires multiple denoising steps (~20 NFE)

### 2.3 GIFSplat — Generative Prior-Guided Iterative Feed-Forward (2026)

GIFSplat represents the most promising hybrid architecture for our use case:

1. **Stage 1 (Feed-Forward)**: Fast coarse 3D Gaussian prediction from sparse unposed views
2. **Stage 2 (Iterative Refinement)**: Small forward-only residual updates guided by distilled diffusion priors
3. **Key property**: Maintains second-scale inference while progressively improving quality

Critical insight: refinement uses **residual updates** (small corrections), not full diffusion denoising. This avoids the NFE bottleneck while leveraging diffusion knowledge.

### 2.4 Difix3D+ — Single-Step Diffusion Cleanup (2025)

A pragmatic approach that works with any 3DGS pipeline:

1. Render novel views from initial 3DGS reconstruction
2. Apply single-step image diffusion model (Difix) to clean up artifacts
3. Use cleaned renders as pseudo-ground-truth for fine-tuning the 3DGS
4. Achieves ~2x FID improvement while maintaining 3D consistency

This is applicable as a **post-processing module** for any feed-forward output.

### 2.5 DiffSplat — Native Gaussian Diffusion (ICLR 2025)

Adapts large-scale text-to-image diffusion models to natively generate 3D Gaussian splat grids:
- Combines diffusion loss on Gaussian splat grids with 3D rendering loss
- Leverages web-scale 2D priors while maintaining 3D consistency
- Scalable to diverse object categories

### 2.6 Known Limitations of Pure Feed-Forward LRMs

Research from 2025-2026 identifies systematic failure modes:

1. **Near-field overfitting**: Excessive Gaussian density near the camera, causing splotchy appearance at close range
2. **Far-field underfitting**: Insufficient coverage in background and distant occluded regions
3. **Resolution trade-off**: High-resolution inputs amplify noise/ghosting; low-resolution inputs lose details
4. **Pose sensitivity**: Quality degrades significantly for unusual poses not well-represented in training data
5. **Temporal inconsistency**: Independent per-frame prediction causes flickering in video sequences

Solutions emerging in 2026:
- **HiRes-GS**: Hierarchical multi-scale optimization with matching-based pruning
- **Long-LRM**: Mamba2 + transformer blocks with token merging for 250K tokens
- **Depth-guided dropout**: Stochastic regularization based on estimated depth uncertainty

## Critical Analysis

### Risk 1: LRM Quality for Back/Occluded Regions

The v5 plan's core assumption — that an LRM can "mathematically hallucinate" high-quality back regions from a single forward pass — is the **highest risk** in the entire architecture.

Evidence against:
- HumanSplat's 0.3s extraction produces coarse geometry for occluded areas
- Complex clothing patterns (e.g., backpack straps continuing to the back) require semantic understanding that single-pass attention may not capture
- Research shows 5-15 dB PSNR gap between visible and occluded regions in feed-forward models

**Mitigation**: Plan for 2-stage inference:
1. Stage 1: Feed-forward LRM (~0.5s) → coarse 360° avatar
2. Stage 2: 1-step refinement (~1-2s) → clean up occluded regions using distilled diffusion

Total latency: ~2-3s — acceptable for avatar creation flow.

### Risk 2: Training Data Requirements for Generalization

LRM generalization depends entirely on training data diversity. Our 90-camera studio rig provides excellent multi-view consistency but limited subject diversity compared to internet-scale datasets.

**Mitigation**:
- Pre-train on large-scale 2D datasets (use diffusion teacher)
- Fine-tune on studio rig data for geometric precision
- Data augmentation: synthetic pose variation, clothing simulation

### Risk 3: Parametric Body Model Dependency

Both v5 and most SOTA methods assume SMPL/SMPL-X for structure priors. This creates:
- Licensing risk (see Section 6.7 — solved by Anny/MHR)
- Topology constraint (SMPL assumes fixed mesh topology — fails for loose clothing, accessories)
- Pose estimation errors propagating to 3D reconstruction

**Mitigation**: Use Anny (Apache 2.0) or MHR (Apache 2.0) as parametric prior. Add a non-parametric branch for clothing/accessories that operates outside the body model topology.

## Architectural Recommendations for MVP

### Recommended Architecture: Two-Stage Hybrid

```
Input (1-3 cameras) → Body Pose Estimator (Anny/MHR)
                           ↓
                    Feature Encoder (DINOv2)
                           ↓
              ┌────────────┴────────────┐
              │                         │
    Voxel-Aligned LRM            Structure Prior
    (coarse 4DGS, ~0.5s)        (body template)
              │                         │
              └────────────┬────────────┘
                           ↓
                    Merge & Render
                           ↓
                  1-Step Refinement
                  (Difix3D+-style, ~1s)
                           ↓
                  Final 4DGS Avatar
```

### Key Design Decisions

| Decision | Choice | Rationale |
|----------|--------|-----------|
| Base generation | Voxel-aligned LRM (not pixel-aligned) | 360° coverage, resolution-independent density |
| Structure prior | Anny or MHR (Apache 2.0) | Commercial safety, competitive quality |
| Refinement | 1-step distilled diffusion | Fixes occluded region artifacts without latency explosion |
| Feature backbone | DINOv2 ViT-L/14 | Best trade-off of semantic richness and speed |
| Training supervision | Rendering loss (L1+LPIPS+DISTS) + depth priors | Proven SOTA approach |
| Total inference budget | <3 seconds on A100 | Acceptable for B2C avatar creation |

## Open Questions

1. **Refinement step count**: Is 1 step sufficient, or do we need 2-4 steps for acceptable quality on complex clothing? Requires ablation study.
2. **Diffusion teacher selection**: SV3D vs Zero123++ vs MVDream for multi-view distillation — which provides best quality/speed for human avatars?
3. **Pose estimation robustness**: How does Anny/MHR perform on unusual body types, children, wheelchair users? Edge cases need testing.
4. **Temporal extension**: GIFSplat is designed for static 3D. How to extend the hybrid paradigm to temporal 4D sequences?
